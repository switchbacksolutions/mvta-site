The Drivers Flat to Ruck-a-Chucky trail follows a 2.5-mile gravel and dirt road descending to the American River at Ruck-a-Chucky on the Middle Fork. This route is a favorite among equestrians thanks to generous off-road trailer parking at the trailhead.

At the river's edge, five primitive campsites managed by Placer County offer a backcountry escape with picnic tables, fire rings, pit toilets, and garbage service — but **no drinking water**, so come prepared. Camping is first come, first served with a 14-day limit.

The access road requires a high-clearance vehicle and may be closed during inclement weather or when seasonal fire restrictions are in effect. Check conditions before heading out.
