The Euchre Bar to Alta trail is a challenging out-and-back route steeped in Gold Rush history. Originally carved by miners in the 19th century, the trail descends steeply from Alta to the North Fork of the American River, continuing to Humbug Creek and back.

The canyon descent features many switchbacks and narrow sections with dramatic 200-foot dropoffs to the river below. At the bottom, a suspension bridge spans the river gorge — a highlight of the route. Scattered remains of original gold mining equipment and the abandoned Old Dorer Mine add historical intrigue along the way.

For those wanting more, the trail continues an additional 5 miles to the Foresthill Divide at Baker Ranch.

**This trail is recommended for serious hikers with wilderness experience.** There is no cell service in the canyon, so download maps before you go and carry plenty of water and food.
