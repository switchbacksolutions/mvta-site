Assassin's Trail is a fascinating and varied 4.5-mile route that follows historic wagon roads through open canyon terrain before climbing a steep section of single-track to a small loop near an overgrown, abandoned garden.

The first two miles travel an easy wagon road with canyon views opening up as you gain elevation. A left turn onto a steeper single-track ascends sharply before reaching a T-junction, where a right turn leads to the route's namesake destination — an eerie abandoned garden linked to an unsolved 1973 cold case murder.

The trail is located on BLM land accessed via Cerro Vista Road and is open to hikers, cyclists, and equestrians. Motorized vehicles are strictly prohibited. Come prepared for poison oak, and be aware that the area is known cougar habitat — avoid hiking alone after dark. Water from the trail-side spring is not safe for human consumption.
