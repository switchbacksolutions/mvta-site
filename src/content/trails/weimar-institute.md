The Weimar Institute Trails offer 300 acres of peaceful paths through wooded terrain on the grounds of a Seventh-day Adventist university and health center. The property's extensive trail network includes the outer Frontier Trail, which traces the perimeter of the campus, plus additional paths along streams and through shaded woodland.

The featured 4.32-mile outer loop is the most popular route, winding through varied terrain with a serene, unhurried atmosphere. Shorter inner paths connect to streams and quieter corners of the property.

The trails are open to hikers, equestrians, and leashed dogs. Mountain bikes are not permitted. Some paths are not accessible to horses — follow all posted signage. After your hike, the on-site Weimar Institute store is worth a stop for healthy refreshments.
