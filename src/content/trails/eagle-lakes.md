Eagle Lakes to Fordyce Creek is a spectacular high-Sierra outing featuring beautiful alpine lakes and streams with breathtaking views of the Sierra Nevada. The route winds through dense forest before opening onto the reflective surfaces of Eagle Lakes at the 3.5-mile mark, with granite peaks rising all around.

From Eagle Lakes, an optional extension continues another 1.5 miles to Fordyce Creek, where a sturdy bridge spans the water and large granite slabs invite a rest in the sun. The full round-trip covers approximately 10.2 miles.

The trail is open to hikers, equestrians, and leashed dogs. Motor vehicles are strictly prohibited beyond the posted boundary. The final approach narrows to single-track and includes small creek crossings, so wear appropriate footwear.
