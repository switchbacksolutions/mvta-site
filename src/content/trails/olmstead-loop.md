The Olmstead Loop Trail — also known as the Knickerbocker Trail — is a quintessential 8.6-mile trek through the rolling oak woodlands and expansive meadows of the Gold Country foothills near Cool, California.

This classic foothill loop rewards visitors with spectacular views of the Sierra Range, wide-open canyon vistas, and seasonal vernal pools teeming with wildflowers in spring. Creek crossings add a sense of adventure, and the moderate terrain makes it accessible to a wide range of trail users.

The trail is open to hikers, mountain bikers, equestrians, and leashed dogs. Because fire roads and cattle paths crisscross the area, **carrying a map is strongly recommended**. Clay soils on portions of the route can become slippery and muddy during and after rain — plan accordingly.
