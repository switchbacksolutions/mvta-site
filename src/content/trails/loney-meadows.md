Loney Meadow is a serene high-elevation area offering one of the region's finest displays of seasonal wildflowers. The flat one-mile meadow loop is suitable for all fitness levels and features interpretive signage about the area's natural and cultural heritage, which stretches from prehistoric use through a historic dairy operation.

The meadow bursts into color in early summer: fawn lilies signal the start of the season in June, followed by a rich succession of camas lilies, sneezeweed, blue-eyed grass, swamp onion, baby elephant heads, and five violet species through mid-July. Birding is excellent throughout the season.

For those seeking more elevation, an additional moderate trail leads from the meadow to a nearby lake. The trailhead is accessed via Bowman Lake Road off Highway 20.
