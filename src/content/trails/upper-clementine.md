Upper Clementine Trail offers one of the most dramatic canyon descents in the Gold Country, dropping 1,150 feet over 4.5 miles from the Cerro Vista Road trailhead down to the shores of Upper Clementine Lake on the North Fork of the American River.

The majority of the descent follows an easy fire road with expansive canyon views at every turn. The final half-mile steepens considerably as the trail drops to the river, where a sandy beach rewards the effort with opportunities for swimming, wading, and picnicking.

At low water, adventurous hikers can ford the river to connect with the 10-mile Foresthill Divide Loop for a longer adventure. A natural spring approximately 2 miles in provides water for horses and dogs — not for humans, so carry your own supply.

The trail is open to hikers, mountain bikers, and equestrians. Motorized vehicles are strictly prohibited. Between mid-November and mid-April the route is restricted to hikers only due to the challenges of wet terrain. Be aware of cougar activity and poison oak throughout the canyon.
